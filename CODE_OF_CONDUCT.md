# ACX Enterprise Project — Code of Conduct
**Version:** 1.0 • **Last Updated:** 2025-08-27

## Our Pledge
We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the ACX Enterprise Project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:
- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Taking responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project’s long-term health.

### Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others’ private information (such as a physical or email address) without explicit permission.
- Violence, threats of violence, doxxing, or encouragement of self-harm.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (including GitHub Issues/PRs/Discussions, mailing lists, documentation, wikis, chat channels, and conferences) and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement Responsibilities
Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Reporting
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it as soon as possible to:

- **Primary:** `conduct@acx.dev`  
- **Security/Emergency (vulnerabilities, threats):** `security@acx.dev`

If you are uncomfortable reporting to the primary contact, you may contact any maintainer individually. Reports are treated as confidential to the extent possible.

### What to include in a report
Please provide:
- Your contact information (optional).
- Names of the individuals involved, including witnesses if possible.
- When and where the incident occurred (links, message IDs, timestamps).
- What happened and whether it is ongoing.
- Any additional context or screenshots.

We will acknowledge receipt of your report within **72 hours** and aim to provide an initial assessment and next steps within **7 days**.

> **Immediate danger:** If you believe someone is in physical danger, call your local emergency number first.

## Enforcement Process & Guidelines
All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The enforcement team will maintain confidentiality to the extent possible and may consult with subject-matter experts or legal counsel when necessary.

### Community Impact Guidelines
**1. Correction**  
*Impact:* Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.  
*Consequence:* A private, written warning and clarification of the Code of Conduct.

**2. Warning**  
*Impact:* A single incident or a series of actions that violate the Code.  
*Consequence:* A warning with consequences for continued behavior. May include temporary restrictions.

**3. Temporary Ban**  
*Impact:* A serious violation or repeated inappropriate behavior.  
*Consequence:* Temporary removal from community spaces and/or contribution privileges for a specified period.

**4. Permanent Ban**  
*Impact:* Demonstrating a pattern of violations, harassment, or aggression; or a single severe incident.  
*Consequence:* Permanent removal from all community spaces and contribution privileges.

### Appeals
If you wish to appeal an enforcement decision, contact `conduct@acx.dev` within **14 days** of the decision. Appeals should include new or clarifying information. The decision on appeal will be final.

## Privacy & Data Handling
- We will keep reporter identities confidential to the extent possible.  
- Minimal records are retained only for as long as necessary to enforce this Code and maintain community safety.  
- Security-related reports are handled under the project’s Security Policy.

## Anti-Retaliation
We prohibit retaliation against any individual who reports a concern in good faith or participates in an investigation.
